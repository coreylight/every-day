Musician of the Day: Chester Thompson

Chester Thompson is a famous drum player from America. He was born on December 11, 1948, in Baltimore, Maryland. He is known for playing with famous music groups like Frank Zappa and The Mothers of Invention, Weather Report, Santana, Genesis and with Phil Collins as a solo artist. Since 2011, he has been performing with his own jazz group called the Chester Thompson Trio. When Chester was a young boy, he learned to play the flute and read music. He started playing the drums when he was 11 years old. He got lessons from a jazz music player named James Harrison. Chester used to practice by playing along with songs by jazz musicians like Miles Davis, Max Roach and Art Blakey. He also studied the music of drummer Elvin Jones. Chester began performing live when he was just a teenager and played his first shows at local places.
