Musician of the Day: Matt King (singer)

Matt King is a country singer and songwriter from Asheville, North Carolina. His mom played piano and his dad, who did lots of cool jobs like being an auctioneer, a stoneworker, and a barber, liked bluegrass guitar. Even though money was tight for him, he kept doing his music and moved to Nashville in 1994. He got to work with a big music company called Atlantic Records, who helped him make his first album in 1997. He even got to perform at a big music show with famous people like Earl Scruggs, Roy Clark, Ricky Skaggs, and Vince Gill. Mr. King has made a few albums. His first one was called "Five O'Clock Hero" and it came out in 1997. He made another one named "Hard Country" in 1999. He also made an album called "Rube" in 2008. All of these albums have his country music songs in them.
