Musician of the Day - Stuart Laughton

Stuart Laughton is a Canadian musician born on August 19, 1951, in St. Catharines, Ontario. He studied trumpet with Joseph Umbrico in Toronto and was a founding member of the Canadian Brass in 1970 before leaving to pursue further studies at the Curtis Institute of Music in Philadelphia. He has established himself as a trumpet concerto soloist and chamber musician and has founded Opening Day Recordings to promote Canadian music and musicians. Laughton rejoined the Canadian Brass in 2003 after a 32-year absence. In addition to his musical career, Laughton was the Founder and Artistic Director of The Forest Festival in Ontario's Haliburton Forest and Wildlife Reserve for 13 years. He is known for his contributions to promoting Canadian music and has had six of the albums he released through Opening Day Recordings nominated for Canadian Juno Awards.
