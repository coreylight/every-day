Musician of the Day: Irma Urrila

Irma Urrila was born on January 29, 1943, in Helsinki. She grew up to be a famous opera singer from Finland. She is really famous around the whole world for singing the part of Pamina in a movie called "The Magic Flute" by Ingmar Bergman in 1975. Apart from being famous for "The Magic Flute", Irma has also sung roles in other big movies. In 1967, she sang the role of Marzelline in a movie version of Beethoven's "Fidelio". She did this during a special opera festival in a place called Savonlinna. Then in 1978, she sang Tatjana in the movie version of Tchaikovsky's "Eugene Onegin".
