Musician of the Day: Mariss Jansons

Mariss Jansons was a very important and famous conductor from Latvia. He was known for conducting music by composers like Mahler, Strauss, Tchaikovsky, Rachmaninoff, and Shostakovich. He was the music director for many big orchestras around the world, including the Bavarian Radio Symphony Orchestra and the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra. Mariss Jansons was born in Riga, Latvia, and moved to Saint Petersburg in 1956 to study music. In his career, he was the music director with the Oslo Philharmonic, the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra, and the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra, among others. He also worked with the London Philharmonic Orchestra and the London Symphony Orchestra often. He won many awards for his work and made lots of recordings.
