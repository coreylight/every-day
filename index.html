Musician of the Day: Ernest Chausson

Ernest Chausson was a very important music composer from France. He was born in 1855 and died in 1899. He grew up in a rich family in Paris. His father wanted him to become a lawyer, so he studied law. But he didn't like it much because he was more interested in music, drawing, and writing. When he was 24 years old, he started studying music at a special school called the Paris Conservatoire. He learned from famous musicians like Jules Massenet and César Franck. He also traveled to places like Bayreuth to listen to the operas of Richard Wagner. He also had five kids with his wife, Jeanne Escudier. He worked as a secretary for a music society until he died. He loved to invite other artists to his house to share ideas and music.
