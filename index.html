Fritz "Freddie" Brocksieper was a German jazz musician, drummer, and bandleader. He was born in Constantinople in 1912 and developed a love for Turkish cymbals at a young age. He began playing professionally in Germany in the 1930s and played with various bands during World War II. Despite having a Jewish mother, he was able to continue playing during the National-Socialist era. Brocksieper continued to perform and lead bands in the post-war years, earning recognition and awards for his work. Freddie Brocksieper was a prominent figure in early European big-band jazz and influenced by Gene Krupa. He recorded with his own ensembles, performed for American GIs, and regularly broadcast live concerts on Bavarian radio. In the 1960s and 1970s, he mainly played in trios and often collaborated with American soloists in Europe. He was awarded a Deutscher Schallplattenpreis in 1980.
