Musician of the Day: Una Mae Carlisle

Una Mae Carlisle was a very talented American singer, pianist, and songwriter who was known for jazz music. She was born on December 26, 1915, in Ohio, and learned to play the piano from her mom. She was so good that she was performing in public by the time she was just three years old! As a teenager, she was discovered by another famous musician, Fats Waller, while she was performing in Cincinnati, Ohio. Later she traveled around Europe giving concerts and even made records. She wrote a song called "Walkin' By The River", which made her the first black woman to have a song on a popular chart. She was also the first black person to have her own national radio show. Lots of famous singers like Cab Calloway and Peggy Lee sang her songs. She passed away in New York in 1956 but her music continues to be loved by many.
