Clem Cattini

Clem Cattini is a British rock and roll drummer who started his career in the late 1950s and 60s. He was a member of The Tornados before becoming a well-known session musician. Cattini has played on hundreds of recordings by various artists, including Cliff Richard and Lou Reed, and has appeared on 42 different UK number one singles. Cattini was born to Italian parents in Stoke Newington, North London. He began his musical journey as a drummer at The 2i's Coffee Bar and then joined the touring band known as the Beat Boys. He later joined Johnny Kidd & the Pirates and became Joe Meek's in-house drummer. Cattini also helped found The Tornados and played on their international hit "Telstar". In 1965, he became a sought-after session musician and has worked with many notable artists throughout his career.
