Jan Savitt on Wikipedia

Jan Savitt was an American musician who was born on September 4, 1907, in a place called Shumsk, which used to be part of Russia. He was very good at playing the violin and even won scholarships to learn more about it. He turned down a big job to keep studying, but then later joined an orchestra led by Leopold Stokowski. For a while, he was a very successful music player and leader of a group of violinists. Jan Savitt had a wife named Barbara and they had two daughters together. He led a band called "Jan Savitt & His Top Hatters" and they played music for a Philadelphia radio station. They also performed with other famous people, like The Andrews Sisters and The Three Stooges, at the Earl Theatre. Jan continued to make music until he passed away in 1948.
