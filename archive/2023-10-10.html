Musician of the Day: Lee Wiley

Lee Wiley was a famous singer from America who loved jazz music. She was born in 1908 in Fort Gibson, Oklahoma, and died in 1975 in New York City. She was active in her singing career from the 1920s to the 1950s. Lee made a decision to be a singer when she was 15 and started singing on New York City radio stations. She also sang on a program for Mrs. Eleanor Roosevelt and had her own show on CBS. She made records of songs by famous people like Gershwin, Cole Porter, and Richard Rodgers & Lorenz Hart. She even wrote some of her own song lyrics with composer Victor Young.
