Musician of the Day: Paul Creston

Paul Creston was an Italian American man who made beautiful classical music. He was born in New York City to parents who came from Sicily. He composed his own music and even though he never had a teacher, he made very special music pieces that were enjoyed by a lot of people. He made many different types of music, like symphonies and concertos, which are long pieces of music that are made for many instruments to play together. He also made music for specific instruments like the violin, piano, marimba, accordion, saxophone and trombone. He even created music inspired by the poetry of Walt Whitman. Creston wasn't just a composer, he taught other people how to make music too. At the end of his life, he lived in Poway, California.
