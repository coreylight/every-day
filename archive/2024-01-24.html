Musician of The Day: Harold Ousley

Harold Lomax Ousley was a famous jazz musician who was born on January 23, 1929, in Chicago, and he died when he was 86 years old in Brooklyn, New York. He was really good at playing the flute and the tenor saxophone. He started playing music in the late-1940s and 1950s and he even got to work with some other famous musicians like Billie Holiday, Gene Ammons, and Dinah Washington. He made his first album in 1961. He didn't make any other albums under his own name after 1977, until the year 2000 when he made "Grit-Grittin' Feelin'". Mr. Ousley wasn't just known for making his own music, he also helped other musicians with their work. He worked with people like George Benson and Jack McDuff during the 1960s. He then played music with Lionel Hampton and Count Basie during the 1970s, all while making his own songs too. Even if he wasn't making his own albums, he still liked to help out on others. He was a very talented man who liked to share his love of music with everyone.
